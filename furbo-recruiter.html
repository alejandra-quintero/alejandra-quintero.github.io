01 · Context

Hardware + Subscription

Furbo Dog Camera sells a pet monitoring device with a free 1-month subscription trial. After that, renewal is optional — and entirely dependent on whether the app feels worth paying for. I joined in 2017 to own brand strategy, app UX, and AI alert integration for Iberoamérica. I identified early that the hardware drove acquisition, but the app drove revenue. Every cold or mistranslated alert was a direct threat to LTV. That insight defined 7 years of product decisions.

02 · The Problem

A Revenue Risk Disguised as a Translation Problem

I identified early that Furbo's recurring revenue depended entirely on one decision: whether the user who got a free month with the camera chose to renew their Furbo Dog Nanny subscription. Expansion into Spanish-speaking markets exposed a structural gap: AI-generated alerts were machine-translated — producing cold, tonally flat messaging with no cultural resonance. The product had no communication system designed for the emotional context of pet ownership in Iberoamérica. Every notification was a moment of truth for the subscription model. And most were failing.

"Every notification was a moment of truth for the business model. The alert had to feel worth paying for — in Spanish, in the right tone, every single day."
03 · What I Did

Four Decisions That Changed Everything

I designed and owned a trust architecture — a system governing how every alert was classified, validated, and delivered. Four decisions defined it:

Decision 01 · Problem Framing
The App Was the Product
The hardware drove acquisition. The subscription drove revenue. I reframed the entire Iberoamérica strategy around that insight — every alert, every localization decision, every quality gate existed to protect one thing: the moment a user decided whether the app was worth paying for.
Decision 02 · System Design
Trust Architecture Over Case-by-Case
Instead of reviewing alerts reactively, I designed a classification system: four tiers (Routine / Sensitive / Health / New Features) each with defined approval workflows, ownership, and a 90%+ perceived clarity threshold. Scalable from day one. No alert shipped below the gate.
Decision 03 · Governance
Authority to Block
I held and exercised veto power over regional launches. When a 2019 Dog Nanny alert tested at 82% clarity — below threshold — I blocked the global rollout over marketing and engineering pressure. Two weeks of iteration. 93% on retest. Zero regional rollback.
Decision 04 · Trade-off
LTV Over Activation
Every pressure in a hardware+subscription model points toward speed: ship fast, activate users, hit the campaign date. I consistently made the opposite call — protecting long-term subscription trust over short-term activation metrics. The 4.7★ rating sustained across 7 years is the compounded result.
